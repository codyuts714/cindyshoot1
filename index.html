"Things Insurance Companies Don't Want You to Know"

Series background Talking head 45–60 sec each
0 / 0

Episode 1: ACV vs. RCV

Script
Hook: "Insurance companies don't want you to know this one thing." Body: "Your policy pays for repairs at replacement cost, but the first check they cut you is almost always based on depreciated value, not what it actually costs to fix. Say you've got a $10,000 roof. They might only offer you $6,000 upfront, because they're only paying you the depreciated value, not the full replacement cost. You're entitled to the rest once the repair is actually done." CTA: "Follow for the next thing they don't want you to know."
Shots
  • Wide establishing shot: 5 sec, cold open / thumbnail frame
  • Talking head, straight-on: full take
  • Cutaway: hands gesturing / flipping a folder

Episode 2: The 30-Day Window

Script
Hook: "They have 30 days. Most people don't know that." Body: "Insurance companies are required to respond to your claim within a set window, in most cases 30 days. If they go silent or stall past that, you have recourse. Don't just wait around and assume nothing can be done." CTA: "Part of the series, follow so you don't miss the next one."
Shots
  • Wide establishing shot: 5 sec, cold open / thumbnail frame
  • Talking head, straight-on: full take
  • Cutaway: hands gesturing / flipping a folder
Keep wardrobe, lighting, and framing identical across both episodes.

"Leak vs. Pipe Burst"

Kitchen → direct-to-camera Cory Anne + Cindy 20–30 sec
0 / 0
Script
Beat 1 (Cory Anne, on the phone): "Hey, yeah, we just have a leak under the sink, it's been dripping for a bit." Beat 2 (text overlay): "'Leak' can mean gradual damage, which is often excluded from coverage." Beat 3 (Cindy, to camera): "Saying 'leak' instead of 'pipe burst' could get your claim denied. The words you use matter. Call a public adjuster before you call your insurance company." Beat 4 (end card text): "Wording matters. Say it right, or call someone who knows how."
